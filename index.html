Caramel Pecan Cinnamon Rolls

Soft, fluffy rolls with a brown-sugar pecan filling and a warm, sticky caramel topping. Perfect for weekends or any time you want the whole place to smell like a bakery.

Source: Adapted for class from Cravings Journal (Lorena Salinas). Original recipe

Sample Imagery

Ingredients

Dough

  • 500 g all-purpose flour
  • 120 g sugar
  • 1½ tsp salt
  • 2¼ tsp instant dry yeast
  • 240 ml warm milk
  • 60 g unsalted butter, melted & cooled
  • 1 egg, room temp
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Filling

  • 60 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 220 g brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 80 g chopped pecans

Salted Caramel Topping

  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 110 g hot heavy cream
  • 50 g unsalted butter (cold, cubed)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 80 g whole pecans

Instructions (short version)

  1. Make the dough: Whisk milk, egg, vanilla, melted butter. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt, yeast. Combine wet + dry and knead just until smooth (it will be sticky). Cover and proof ~1 hour until doubled.
  2. Roll + fill: On a floured surface, roll to ~12x16 in (30x40 cm). Brush with melted butter; sprinkle cinnamon sugar and chopped pecans.
  3. Shape: Roll up from the long side, slice thick rolls, place in a lined pan with a bit of space between. Proof ~30 min.
  4. Bake: 350°F / 180°C for 20-30 min, until lightly golden.
  5. Caramel: Melt sugar dry-style to light amber. Carefully whisk in hot cream (it will bubble), then butter + salt; stir in pecans.
  6. Finish: Pour caramel over warm rolls. Cool a few minutes before serving.
